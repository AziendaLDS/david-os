Zinc & Copper Balance

Zinc

RDA ~11mg/day for adult men. Upper limit ~40mg from all sources combined
Supports testosterone, immune function, skin healing, muscle recovery — easy to under-hit on carb-heavy diets
From food: eggs (~1.1mg/2), lentils (~2.5mg/cup), pumpkin seeds (~2.2mg/oz) → roughly 5–6mg/day from your usual foods
Supplement: 15mg picolinate or bisglycinate, with food in the AM. Lands total ~20–21mg/day — comfortably mid-range

Copper

RDA ~0.9mg/day. Zinc and copper compete for absorption — sustained zinc dosing can deplete copper over months
Sources already in your diet: cashews, sesame, lentils/chickpeas, dark chocolate, whole grains
Healthy ratio for supplementers ~8–15:1 zinc:copper — keeping copper foods in rotation covers this
If you get bloodwork, check copper alongside zinc once you've been supplementing a few months
Avoid Before Bed
Heavy protein meals
Takes energy to digest, keeps your system activated when it should wind down. Save big protein for lunch/early dinner.
Alcohol
Helps you fall asleep but destroys quality in the second half of the night. Suppresses REM. You wake unrestored.
Spicy or very fatty food
Raises core temperature and causes reflux. Your body needs to cool to initiate deep sleep — this fights it.
Caffeine after 2pm
5–6hr half-life means a 3pm espresso still has half its caffeine at 8pm. Delays sleep onset significantly.
05 Meal Timing & Daily Anchors
Fasted until noon, ~8–9hr eating window. Each meal has to be a real meal — under-eating at noon undercuts the whole day's energy, and the fasting window must not quietly become overall under-eating given the training load.
7AM wake · fasted until noon

Black, or a splash of milk. Good window for morning light + prehab since you're not tied to a meal.

Main fuel-up after ~17hrs fasted, substantial not light. Eggs/chicken thighs + whole grain carb + a vegetable. Best window for citrus/berries and any guilty-pleasure carbs.

Pumpkin seeds or cashews, or carrots/cucumber + hummus. A small carb (banana) if training at 5pm.

7:30–8:00 PM

Chicken, fish, or lean beef + vegetable-heavy side. Lighter on dense carbs than noon, but don't under-eat — this is recovery.
8:30–9:00 PM

Skip if satisfied. If not, small oats/banana or fruit fits the 3-hour-before-bed buffer.
9:00 PM

Sleep math: 7:00am wake with a 7–7.5hr need means lights out 11:30pm–12:00am. 11:30 is the real target, midnight the hard ceiling. Treat bedtime as seriously as wake time — one without the other anchors nothing.

🔍 Why You Still Break Out

Skincare manages skin — it doesn't override hormonal cycles. Occasional breakouts are normal
Heavy products on nights skin doesn't need them can irritate the barrier — more isn't always better
Pimple patches only work on active whiteheads with material in them — not useful if you don't get classic pimples
Milia (white dots): retinol breaks them down over 6–8 weeks. Don't squeeze. The wait is the timeline, not failure
